The Temple of Apollo Palatinus was a temple to the god Apollo in Rome, constructed on the Palatine Hill on the initiative of Augustus between 36 and 28 BCE. It was associated with Augustus 's victories at the battles of Naulochus and Actium; the latter was extensively memorialised through its decoration.
